Brand Identity - Muro - 3/2012

The concept of Muro (meaning 'wall' in spanish) was centered around decorating walls with posters. The client would give Muro a wall and a theme (which could be an artist, a team, a country - pretty much anything) and Muro's team would decorate the wall with different sizes of posters of different styles, in sync with the theme.

Whenever one designs a logo, it should be considered that a logo can be used in a lot of different spaces. Other than being used on a website, business card etc, it can also be used in challenging places like in the sponsors section of a poster, where it will be given hardly 1"x1" space and it should fit there and be legible. A logo might also need to be printed on big billboards, scaled to sizes upto several feets. In that case, the logo shouldn't look featureless and bland. A logo might need to be printed/carved on metal/wood surfaces, where only 1 color can be used. So the logo should retain its character even in only 1 color.

Muro's logo needed to be neutral, since it could be printed on the posters in such a way that it doesn't distort or overpower the poster, but is still there for people to see. I wanted the logo to give a feeling of completion, but not very symmetrical or perfect, just the way different sized posters cover a wall. Muro's logo is one of the few designs that I still like as much as I did when I created it and is my favorite (out of 4-5) logos that I've created this far.
